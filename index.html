Ariyo Imanuel Tarigan
PhD student at University of the Ryukyus

I’m currently working in coral taxonomy, with a special interest in how hybridization and introgression shape the relationships between different coral species. I’m fascinated by how gene flow influences their diversity and ability to adapt to changing environments. I also study how coral gametes recognize each other during spawning, which helps us understand how species boundaries are maintained—or sometimes crossed—in the wild. Altogether, my work aims to shed light on how corals evolve, interact, and persist in today’s rapidly shifting oceans.
